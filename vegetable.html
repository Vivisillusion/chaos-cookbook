3. Vegetable soup

This is a recipe I first learned while working at a hotel kitchen. They used to serve it with a fresh loaf of bread and a generous sprinkle of parmesan. It's simple, comforting, and always feels like coming home from the rain.

Ingredients

  • Pumpkin, diced
  • Carrots, chopped
  • Parsnip, peeled and sliced
  • Potatoes, cubed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Olive oil (or your preferred oil)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water (enough to cover the vegetables)
  • Parmesan cheese and bread for serving (optional, but magical)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat a bit of oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped vegetables and season lightly with salt and pepper. Stir and let them sauté for a minute or two—just enough to wake them up.
  3. Pour in enough water to cover all the vegetables. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until everything is tender, about 20–25 minutes.
  4. Turn off the heat. Use a blender or immersion blender to mix the soup until smooth and creamy.
  5. Serve warm with crusty bread and a sprinkle of parmesan, if you like. It’s nourishing, humble, and a little bit hotel-luxury.
